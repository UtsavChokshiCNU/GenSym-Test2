Configuring Delivery Product Parameters: Base Manufacturer

You configure inventory and planning parameters for the delivery products of each supplier role in the model. You also configure parameters related to selling price, published lead time, batch size, and manufacturing yield.

The Order Type of the delivery product determines the planning strategy a role uses for making and delivery products-stock, make-to-order, or engineer-to-order. The role uses make planning to determine the size of each build order and when the role manufactures each build order.

Normally, the upstream suppliers' delivery product determines the price buyers must pay for their source products. However, because a Base Manufacturer role has no upstream suppliers, it determines the price it pays for its source products, based on the Purchase Cost parameter, which you configure on the Source tab of the delivery product.

Note: Normally, the Purchase Cost is a metric that the model computes for buyer roles in the model; however, for a Base Manufacturer role only, Purchase Cost is a parameter that you configure.

If the role manufactures multiple delivery products, you can configure the Product Preference for each delivery product when choosing orders for shipment, based on preference.

If the role is one of multiple suppliers for the same delivery product to a single downstream buyer, you can configure the Supplier Preference for each delivery product when choosing suppliers based on contracts.

If the role sources, makes, or delivers multiple products, you can configure various multipliers for each source and delivery product to model variation in timing parameters for each product. The simplest e-SCOR model sources and delivers a single product only; therefore, you do not need to configure these multipliers.

Configure the delivery product parameters on the following tabs for the Base Manufacturer role so that it:

Product tab:
- Uses a stock planning strategy for making and delivering the product family, the default.
Inventory tab:
- Has 100 units of the product family in inventory when the model starts.
- Can hold a maximum of 1000 product families in inventory.
Source tab:
- Costs $100 for raw materials to manufacture the product family.
Manufacture tab:
- Manufactures delivery products with a maximum batch size of 50 units and a minimum batch size of 30 units.
- Has a 95% yield during the manufacturing process.
- Adjusts the Total Build Time of the Make category to be proportional to the size of the build order.
Delivery tab:
- Manufactures complete finished products only (Complete Only), the default; does not assemble components into kits (Assembled) or outsource complete finished products (Complete).
- Charges $250 as the net selling price for each product family it delivers to buyers.
- Tells buyers that it can turn around orders for product families in 5 days.
Planning tab:
- Creates replenishment orders for product families when the inventory level drops below 50 units, given current orders.
- Creates build orders for a minimum of 10 units when creating replenishment orders.
- Tries to maintain 500 units of product families in inventory when determining build order size, based on make planning.
